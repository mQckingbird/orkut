Journal Scope

One morning, when Gregor Samsa woke from troubled dreams, he found himself transformed in his bed into a horrible vermin. He lay on his armour-like back, and if he lifted his head a little he could see his brown belly, slightly domed and divided by arches into stiff sections. The bedding was hardly able to cover it and seemed ready to slide off any moment. His many legs, pitifully thin compared with the size of the rest of him, waved about helplessly as he looked. "What's happened to me?" he thought. It wasn't a dream. His room, a proper human room although a little too small, lay peacefully between its four familiar walls. A collection of textile samples lay spread out on the table - Samsa was a travelling salesman - and above it there hung a picture that he had recently cut out of an illustrated magazine and housed in a nice, gilded frame. It showed a lady fitted out with a fur hat and fur boa who sat upright, raising a heavy fur muff that covered the whole of her lower arm towards the viewer. Gregor then turned to look out the window at the dull weather.

    Guidelines

    • SINCE AUG 2011, ALL FIGURES AND MULTIMEDIA APPENDICES ARE NOW GENERATED AND INSERTED IN THE FINAL MANUSCRIPT FROM THE DATABASE. THIS INCLUDES THE FIGURE CAPTIONS. Only edit figure captions in the database. During the review process, leave figures and captions also in the manusscript, only remove them during copyediting.
    • Our system/typesetter will insert figures after the paragraph where the figure is first mentioned in the text. Each figure must therefore be mentioned in the text (e.g. "Figure 1 illustrates this, FIgure 2 shows that...")
    • Do not use Figure 1a, 1b etc. If figure parts make sense (because they are very small) then author MUST provide ONE figure file with all the figure parts a part of one figure (e.g. merge the figure parts into one figure). If figure parts are new figures, they must be renumbered (Fig 1, 2, 3, ..)
    • FIGURE NUMBERS AND CAPTION MUST NOT BE PART OF THE IMAGE (if they are, authors will be asked to remove the caption from the image file and have to upload a new image file as supplementary file)
    • Figure headings in the database/form field "caption" MUST NOT INCLUDE figure label/numbers like '''Figure 1''', '''Figure 2''' etc -these will be added automatically during typesetting. If you add "Figure 1" to the caption, then this will result in a duplicate label ("Figure 1. Figure 1..").
    • For the caption, use sentence case (NOT ALL CAPS, Not Title Case, not all small letters) with a period at the end (NOTE: Until Aug 24th JMIR figure captions had no periods at the end. As this is contrary to AMA style, captions from manuscripts copyedited after Aug 24th 2011 must have a period at the end).
    • DO NOT include notes at the bottom of a figure (they must be included in the caption or the text). Figures have no footnotes.
    • Captions should be succinct. Lengthy explanations should be moved to the body of the manuscript. On the other hand, each figure should be designed so that it can be easily understood by the reader without consulting the main body of the manuscript.
